English Tenses

Understanding tenses is key to mastering English grammar. Tenses tell us when an action takes place - in the past, present, or future. Each tense has different forms to show whether an action is simple, continuous, perfect, or perfect continuous. Let's explore the 12 main tenses in English and how to use them correctly.

The 12 Basic Tenses in English

🔄 Present Tenses
Tense Structure Example Usage
Present Simple Subject + V1 (+ s/es) I speak. She speaks. Habits, facts, general truths
Present Continuous Subject + am/is/are + V-ing I am speaking now. Actions happening now or temporary situations
Present Perfect Subject + have/has + V3 I have spoken to her. Past actions with present relevance
Present Perfect Continuous Subject + have/has been + V-ing I have been speaking for an hour. Actions that started in past and continue to present
Present Simple Examples

The sun rises in the east. (general truth)

She works as a doctor. (permanent situation)

I play tennis every Sunday. (habit)

Present Continuous Examples

I am learning English grammar right now. (happening now)

She is staying with her parents this week. (temporary situation)

They are coming to dinner tonight. (future arrangement)

Present Perfect Examples

I have traveled to five countries. (past experience with present relevance)

She has lived here since 2020. (action started in past and continues)

They have already finished their homework. (completed action with relevance now)

Present Perfect Continuous Examples

I have been studying English for six years. (ongoing action from past to present)

She has been working here since January. (emphasis on duration)

They have been waiting for the bus for 30 minutes. (continuous action until now)

⏮️ Past Tenses
Tense Structure Example Usage
Past Simple Subject + V2 I spoke yesterday. Completed actions in the past
Past Continuous Subject + was/were + V-ing I was speaking when you called. Actions in progress at a specific time in past
Past Perfect Subject + had + V3 I had spoken before you arrived. Actions completed before another past action
Past Perfect Continuous Subject + had been + V-ing I had been speaking for hours. Continuous actions that happened before another past action
Past Simple Examples

She visited Paris last summer. (completed action)

They lived in New York for ten years. (finished period)

I bought a new car yesterday. (specific time in past)

Past Continuous Examples

I was watching TV when the phone rang. (interrupted action)

They were working all day yesterday. (action in progress)

At 8 PM last night, we were having dinner. (specific time in past)

Past Perfect Examples

By the time I arrived, the train had left. (action before another past action)

She had studied for six hours before she took the exam. (finished action before another)

They had never seen such a beautiful sunset before that evening. (experience before a point in past)

Past Perfect Continuous Examples

I had been working for five hours when you called. (duration before another action)

She had been living in London before she moved to Paris. (continuous action before another)

They had been studying English for years before they visited the UK. (ongoing action before past event)

⏭️ Future Tenses
Tense Structure Example Usage
Future Simple Subject + will + V1 I will speak tomorrow. Predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions
Future Continuous Subject + will be + V-ing I will be speaking at 8 PM. Actions in progress at a specific future time
Future Perfect Subject + will have + V3 I will have spoken by then. Actions that will be completed before a future time
Future Perfect Continuous Subject + will have been + V-ing I will have been speaking for 2 hours. Continuous actions that will be completed by a future time
Future Simple Examples

I will help you with your homework. (promise/offer)

It will rain tomorrow. (prediction)

I think she will pass the exam. (opinion about future)

Future Continuous Examples

This time next week, I will be sitting on a beach in Hawaii. (action at specific future time)

She will be studying when you arrive. (ongoing action in future)

They will be flying to Paris at this time tomorrow. (action in progress)

Future Perfect Examples

By next month, I will have finished this project. (completed before future point)

She will have lived here for ten years by 2026. (duration up to future point)

They will have built the bridge by the time we visit again. (completed future action)

Future Perfect Continuous Examples

By December, I will have been teaching for 20 years. (ongoing action up to future point)

Next week, she will have been working on this project for six months. (emphasis on duration)

By the time you arrive, they will have been waiting for three hours. (duration until future event)
